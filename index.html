Drip Irrigation – Complete Guide

🌱 What is Drip Irrigation?

Drip irrigation delivers water directly to plant roots through a network of pipes, tubes, and emitters.

✅ Key Benefits

  • Water saving: 40-60% compared to flood irrigation
  • Reduced weed growth – water only reaches crop roots
  • Fertilizer can be mixed with water (fertigation)
  • Suitable for all soil types, especially sandy & loamy
  • Govt subsidy up to 55% under PMKSY scheme

💰 Cost Breakdown

Main pipeline & fittings₹8,000 - ₹12,000
Drip laterals & emitters₹10,000 - ₹15,000
Filters & valves₹4,000 - ₹6,000

🌾 Best Crops for Drip Irrigation

Sugarcane, Cotton, Grapes, Pomegranate, Onion, Tomato, Banana, Papaya, and all vegetable crops. Not recommended for rice or other flood-tolerant crops.

☁️

Sprinkler Irrigation – Complete Guide

🌧️ What is Sprinkler Irrigation?

Sprinkler systems spray water into the air like natural rainfall, covering large areas uniformly.

✅ Key Benefits

  • Water saving: 30-40% compared to flood irrigation
  • Uniform water distribution across the field
  • Ideal for undulating/uneven terrain
  • Suitable for wheat, pulses, oilseeds, and fodder crops
  • Reduces soil compaction

💰 Cost Breakdown

ComponentCost (₹/acre)
Pump & motor₹8,000 - ₹15,000
HDPE pipes₹6,000 - ₹10,000
Sprinkler heads & couplers₹5,000 - ₹8,000
Installation₹3,000 - ₹5,000
Total (approx)₹22,000 - ₹38,000

🌾 Best Crops for Sprinkler

Wheat, Jowar, Bajra, Groundnut, Soybean, Pulses, Oilseeds, Fodder crops, and Lawns. Can also be used for cooling in grape vineyards.

🏞️

Canal Irrigation – Complete Guide

🚜 What is Canal Irrigation?

Canal irrigation uses a network of canals to divert water from rivers, dams, or reservoirs to fields.

✅ Key Information

  • Major canal systems: Mula-Mutha, Godavari, Krishna, Tapi
  • Best suited for: Sugarcane, Rice, Wheat, Cotton
  • Low operational cost once infrastructure is built
  • Dependent on dam water levels and rainfall
  • Water rotation schedule (पाणी पाळी) system is common
🌧️

Rainwater Harvesting – Complete Guide

💦 Why Rainwater Harvesting?

Maharashtra faces frequent droughts. Rainwater harvesting captures monsoon runoff for dry months.

🏗️ Methods

  • Farm Pond (शेततळे): Excavate a pond to store runoff – Govt subsidy available
  • Check Dam (बंधारा): Small dam across streams to recharge groundwater
  • Rooftop Harvesting: Collect roof water in tanks for irrigation
  • Contour Bunding: Earthen embankments along contours

Soil Analysis Guide

Black Soil (काळी माती)

Rich in clay, retains moisture well. Best for: Cotton, Sugarcane, Wheat, Jowar, Tur. Found in: Vidarbha, Marathwada, parts of Khandesh.

Red Soil (लाल माती)

Well-drained, slightly acidic. Best for: Groundnut, Bajra, Tur, Vegetables. Found in: Konkan, parts of Nashik, Pune.

Loamy Soil (गाळाची माती)

Ideal mix of sand, silt & clay. Best for: Most vegetables, Soybean, Maize, Grapes. Found in: River valleys across MH.

Clay Soil (चिकण माती)

Heavy, water-retentive. Best for: Rice, Jowar. Found in: Konkan, canal-irrigated areas.

Crop Rotation Guide

Why Rotate Crops?

Crop rotation breaks pest cycles, improves soil structure, and balances nutrient usage. Different crops use different nutrients, preventing soil depletion.

Rotation Pattern 1

Cotton → Soybean → Wheat
(Kharif → Kharif → Rabi)

Rotation Pattern 2

Rice → Tur → Groundnut
(Kharif → Kharif → Rabi)

Rotation Pattern 3

Sugarcane → Onion → Maize
(Annual → Rabi → Zaid)

Rotation Pattern 4

Bajra → Tomato → Jowar
(Kharif → Rabi → Rabi)

NPK Fertilizer Guide

N – Nitrogen

Promotes leaf & stem growth. Sources: Urea (46% N), Ammonium Sulphate. Apply at sowing & vegetative stage.

P – Phosphorus

Essential for root development & flowering. Sources: DAP (18-46-0), SSP. Apply at sowing time.

K – Potassium

Increases disease resistance & grain quality. Sources: MOP (60% K), Potash. Apply at flowering stage.

Recommended Ratios

N:P:K = 4:2:1 for most crops. Always soil test before application. Excess fertilizer harms soil & groundwater.

Organic Farming Guide

🌱 Organic Inputs

  • Vermicompost (गांडूळ खत): 2 tons/acre – Rich in nutrients
  • Green Manure: Dhaincha, Sunhemp – Plow into soil before sowing
  • Neem Cake: Natural pest repellent + fertilizer
  • Jeevamrut: Cow dung + cow urine + jaggery + gram flour ferment
  • Panchagavya: 5 cow products blend for plant health

Crop Disease Guide

🌾 Wheat Rust (गव्हाचा तांबेरा)

Symptoms: Orange-brown pustules on leaves & stems. Control: Spray Propiconazole fungicide. Use resistant varieties like HD-3086.

🌱 Cotton Bollworm (कापसाची बोंडअळी)

Symptoms: Larvae bore into bolls, damaging fiber. Control: Neem-based pesticide, pheromone traps, Bt cotton varieties.

🍚 Rice Blast (भाताचा करपा)

Symptoms: Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves. Control: Apply Carbendazim or Tricyclazole at tillering stage.

🧅 Onion Purple Blotch (कांद्याचा जांभळा ठिपका)

Symptoms: Purple spots on leaves, bulb rot. Control: Mancozeb spray at 10-day intervals. Crop rotation helps.

🍅 Tomato Early Blight (टोमॅटोचा अर्ली ब्लाईट)

Symptoms: Dark concentric rings on leaves. Control: Copper oxychloride spray. Remove infected leaves.

Government Schemes for Farmers

💰 PM-KISAN Yojana

₹6,000 per year direct income support to farmer families. Paid in 3 installments of ₹2,000 each. All small & marginal farmers eligible.

🛡️ PMFBY (Crop Insurance)

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana – Low premium crop insurance. Covers losses due to drought, floods, pests. Premium: 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi.

💧 PMKSY (Irrigation)

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana – Up to 55% subsidy for drip & sprinkler installation. Har khet ko pani mission.

🏦 Maharashtra Baliraja Scheme

Loan waiver & support for distressed farmers. Interest-free crop loans up to ₹3 lakh through Kisan Credit Card.

🏦

Loan & Subsidy Information

Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

Low-interest loans up to ₹3 lakh. Interest rate: 4% (with 2% subvention). Repayment after harvest. Available at all nationalized banks.

Farm Mechanization Subsidy

40-60% subsidy on tractors, rotavators, harvesters. Apply through state agriculture department portal.

NABARD Schemes

Long-term loans for irrigation, dairy, poultry. Special schemes for women & SC/ST farmers. Contact nearest NABARD office.

Interest Subvention

2% interest relief on timely crop loan repayment. Additional 3% for prompt payers – effective rate becomes 4%.
